Bio

I'm currently a Master's student in the David R. Cheriton School of Computing Science at the University of Waterloo, where I'm co-supervised by Edith Law and Kate Larson. My broad research interests are in modelling people's beliefs, and using these models to develop better AI systems. My projects at Waterloo have specifically focused on group-based tasks on crowdsourcing systems and formalizing people's thoughts on this work with cooperative game theory.
Before Waterloo, I earned a B.Eng. in Computer Engineering from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Dalhousie University. My work experience from their co-op program includes:
  • Developing inexpensive, open-source tools for cryptographic side channel analysis with NewAE Technology
  • Finding analytical solutions for head impact models with the Dalhousie Department of Engineering Mathematics
  • Building battery test equipment for the Jeff Dahn Research Group, which was used to research novel Li-ion battery chemistries
